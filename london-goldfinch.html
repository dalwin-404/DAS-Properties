Property Details

Located in a major area of regeneration, Hendon Waterside is a new residential and award-winning community in North London; 2,000 homes across 30 acres. Regeneration zones like this one are noted for their huge impact on residential property prices. According to recent research by CBRE, they attract an average price growth of 3.6% per year more than other properties. Hendon Waterside is a key part of the regeneration of West Hendon alongside neighbouring Brent Cross and Hampstead. Already 1,000 homes have been built. First wave investors in 2012 have seen their apartments increase in value by 132% over 10 years.

The Goldfinch Apartments are the latest release from Barratt. The property prices start at £392,000 with only a 10% deposit needed to buy and exceptionally strong yields for London at 6%, presenting an incredible opportunity to buy in this new residential community. Hendon Waterside enjoys a broad tenant pool; young professionals, families attached by good local schools and also students with it being ideally commutable to London’s top universities.

LOCATION

What makes this development so special is that it fronts a natural reservoir – both a Site of Special Scientific Interest and a local nature reserve, quite remarkable in a city of London's size. There are 170 hectares of open water surrounded by footpaths, trails, and woods, home to several local sailing clubs and other water sports. Hendon Station is only a five-minute walk, from which St Pancras International Station is a 16min commute, whilst Hendon Central Underground is a 16 min walk, granting access to the Northern Line – now directly connected to the new Battersea Power Station. For car owners, its appeal is further cemented by the fact you can be on the M1 heading north out of town in a matter of minutes.

NW9 is a popular postcode, not only with young professionals for its less than 30-minute commute to the heart of The City and Central London but also for young families seeking to capitalise on the neighbourhood's excellent primary and secondary schools. Following the post-Covid shift in lifestyles, this location ticks all the boxes – easy access to acres of greenspace whilst being a short commute to key locations in Central London and The City.

Brent Cross shopping centre is another draw – a major retail and leisure destination and one of London's most popular malls with a Waitrose, John Lewis, Fenwick's, and other favoured high street brands and restaurants.
