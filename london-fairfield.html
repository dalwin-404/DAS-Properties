Property Details

Fairfield Lodge is a collection of 4 luxury three-bedroom apartments in a peaceful cul-de-sac in the upmarket village of Cobham; one of Surrey’s most desirable locations often known for being the home of Chelsea Football Club’s training grounds.

Adjacent to serene woodlands, with all the conveniences of Cobham’s high street nearby, these are residences that blend a town and country experience. Residents enjoy direct access to open spaces with walking trails immediately accessible – and a good lunch at The Ivy afterwards, just down the road.

Excellent local schooling, an active community and good public transport connections, a 38-minute train ride into Central London, give this investment sound appeal, either as a buy-to-let or second home prospect.

The principle bedrooms all feature dressing rooms and ensuites and the remaining two bedrooms all with ensuites also offer the opportunity to customise one as a home office or playroom. That every bedroom also has a bathroom increases their rental premium along with the provision of private, off-street parking.

Cobham directly benefits from the ‘Southwest London effect’ whereby homeowners and renters looking to escape higher prices in the capital have driven up demand in the Surrey village. There is a documented shortage of new build rental property in Cobham, part of Elmbridge Borough, dubbed the ‘least affordable place to live in the country’ because of its reputation as an enclave for wealthy residents. This makes Fairfield Lodge an ideal property investment, underpinned by strong long-term capital appreciation in a coveted and perennially popular area to live.

The coming years will only witness more spill over from London, driving prices up along the Surrey-London commuter belt and therefore we’re delighted to offer our investors this rare and exclusive opportunity to buy one of only four top-spec apartments.

LOCATION

Woolwich was established as a Royal Dockyard in the early 1500s and became the location for the Royal Arsenal, now enjoying its modern incarnation as a vibrant cultural hub. Part of the borough of Royal Greenwich, it has undergone intense gentrification over the last decade and its preservation of its heritage architecture whilst developing as a modern neighbourhood is often hailed as one of the best regeneration schemes in London. In 2023 it was named by The Sunday Times as one of the best places to live.

The neighbourhood has developed a strong reputation for arts and culture, home to Woolwich Works an emerging destination for music, art and comedy, museums and the shopping and dining of Spray Street Quarter. Since it was identified as an Opportunity Area by the Major of London in 2004, house prices have grown by over 91%. And there is still plenty of opportunity for growth.

The Elizabeth Line has been yet another boon to growth, accelerating regeneration and further large-scale investment, topping £1billion from the private sector. Property prices in Woolwich are expected to grow by 19.4% between 2025-2029 (Savills, JLL), whilst apartment growth figures over the last decade have outperformed those in neighbouring boroughs by 49%.
